--- title: InkChat, a collaborative digital ink web app layout: posts categories: projects permalink: /projects/inkchat --- In summer 2014, I took on a software development position at Western University under Dr. Stephen Watt. We wanted to create a collaborative digital drawing environment for the web that was both real-time and efficient.

The result was InkChat.




InkChat allows for real-time streaming of digital ink traces, like the ones above. A trace is defined as a contiguous mark that begins at the canvas position where a pen, finger, or mouse click first occurs, and ends where that action is released. Each trace is sent individually using Socket.IO, represented in InkML as a series of discrete (x,y) points. This allows the sending of ink traces between clients in a room to be pretty efficient.


I was responsible for implementing many of the front-end components, including the drawing and erasing features, and integration of the Fabric.js library to allow for trace resizing and manipulation. I also helped to develop an image upload tool, which, among other things, can be used to draw on burrito cats. :D
